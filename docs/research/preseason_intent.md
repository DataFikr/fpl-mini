## 5. Three Biggest Recurring Structural Problems

### Problem 1: "How do I build a GW1 squad when half my premiums played the World Cup final?"

**The pain:** The 2026 World Cup final is ~4 weeks before GW1. Managers face a structural dilemma — load up on premium assets who may be rested/managed, or pivot to differentials who skipped the tournament. The community is split between "back the talent" and "wait and see with a GW3 wildcard."

**Why it's structural:** It's not a one-player question. It affects 5-6 squad slots simultaneously (Haaland, Saka, Odegaard, Bruno, Van Dijk, Wirtz). Getting the template wrong means burning an early transfer or wildcard.

**Recurring thread titles:**
- "Is it worth going without Haaland GW1?"
- "GW1 template without World Cup players?"
- "Best structure if you skip all fatigued premiums"
- "When to wildcard after World Cup — GW2, GW3, or GW4?"

### Problem 2: "How do I value new signings with zero Premier League data?"

**The pain:** Wirtz (Liverpool, £116M) is the headline case, but every window brings 2-3 marquee arrivals with no PL history. FPL tools rely on historical PL data — xG models, fixture-adjusted projections, price predictions — none of which work for players from the Bundesliga, La Liga, or Serie A. Managers are flying blind on the biggest squad decision of the preseason.

**Why it's structural:** Price is set by FPL Towers based on reputation, not PL output. Community debates whether Wirtz is a £10M bargain or a £10M trap. No existing tool translates cross-league xG/xA into FPL-projected points-per-million.

**Recurring thread titles:**
- "Florian Wirtz FPL price — worth it or wait?"
- "How to value new signings with no PL stats"
- "Wirtz vs Salah vs Diaz — Liverpool midfield picks"
- "Best new-signing FPL picks 2026/27"

### Problem 3: "Which promoted team assets are actually worth targeting?"

**The pain:** Coventry City, Ipswich Town (returning after one year in Championship), and Hull City are promoted. Every season, 1-2 promoted team players become surprise FPL gems at 4.5-5.5M, but the community has no reliable way to identify them before GW1. FDR ratings for new teams are guesswork. Budget enablers from promoted sides are the difference between affording 3 premiums or 2.

**Why it's structural:** Budget structure depends on finding 4.5M defenders and 5.0M midfielders who actually start. Promoted teams are the primary source. Getting this wrong cascades into the entire squad build.

**Recurring thread titles:**
- "Best 4.5M defenders from promoted teams?"
- "Any promoted team attackers worth a punt?"
- "Coventry/Ipswich/Hull — who's nailed?"
- "Fixture difficulty for promoted teams 2026/27"
